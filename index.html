Visualisations

All 7 research questions

Built with ggplot2 in R. Charts below are interactive reproductions of the original plots, built from the actual dataset structure.

Q 01

ESCS Distribution by Country

Median and spread of socioeconomic status across 16 selected countries. Width of box shows internal inequality; position shows relative wealth.

geom_boxplot

Q 02

ESCS Trend 2012–2018

Top 5 and bottom 5 countries by 2018 ESCS score. Tracks whether socioeconomic gaps are widening or narrowing over time.

geom_line + geom_point

Q 03

ESCS vs Parental Education

Does parental education predict overall ESCS? Separate regression lines for OECD and non-OECD groups reveal structural differences.

geom_point + geom_smooth(lm)

Q 04

Within-Country Inequality

Violin plots reveal the shape of ESCS distributions. A bimodal or flat shape signals a socioeconomic split within a country's student population.

geom_violin + geom_jitter

Q 05

Global ESCS Map (2018)

Choropleth showing mean ESCS per country. Grey = no PISA participation. Clear regional patterns: Northern Europe and East Asia lead; Sub-Saharan Africa and parts of Latin America lag.

geom_sf · rnaturalearth

Q 06

OECD vs Non-OECD: Density

Overlapping density curves by group and cycle. Dashed lines mark group means. Shows distributional shift and narrowing/widening of the gap.

geom_density · facet_wrap(cycle)

Q 07

ESCS Component Correlations

Correlation matrix of the four index components. Reveals which factor — parental occupation, education, or home resources — drives the composite score most.

ggcorrplot · geom_tile

Methodology

Tools & techniques

Each visualisation was built with a specific geom tailored to the research question. All charts share a unified theme for visual consistency.

Data manipulation

  • Grouped summaries with group_by() + summarise()
  • Missing value handling via drop_na() and na.rm
  • Stratified sampling with slice_sample(n = 50 000) for performance
  • Factor reordering with fct_reorder() for sorted axes
  • Cycle recoding: numeric → year labels via case_when()
  • ISO country code joining with rnaturalearth sf object

Visualisation techniques

  • Boxplots with outlier transparency for large-N legibility
  • Multi-line trend chart with direct labelling (ggrepel)
  • Scatter with dual regression bands by group (geom_smooth(lm))
  • Violin + crossbar median overlay + alpha jitter
  • Choropleth map via geom_sf + viridis mako scale
  • Faceted density with group mean vlines
  • Circle-size correlation matrix (ggcorrplot)
